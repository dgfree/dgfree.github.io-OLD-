Question 3

25 Mar 2015

Question: If $V$ and $W$ are 2-dimensional subspaces of $\mathbb{R}^4$, what are the possible dimensions of the subspace $V \cap W$?

$A)$ 1 only

$B)$ 2 only

$C)$ 0 and 1 only

$D)$ 0, 1, and 2 only

$E)$ 0, 1, 2, 3, and 4

Answer: There are three cases to consider for the problem. In the case where $V = W$, then the subspace $V \cap W$ is equivalent to $V$ and to $W$, so $\dim V \cap W = \dim V = \dim W = 2$.

In the case where $V$ and $W$ share exactly one basis vector, then the intersection will only contain one basis vector and $\dim V \cap W = 1$.

Otherwise, if $V$ and $W$ share no vectors, then the intersection is the zero vector space $\{0\}$, which is defined to have dimension $0$.
